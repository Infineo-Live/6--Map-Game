# Game Logic Flow

## Core Concept

The player helps Ravan travel toward Lanka by exploring a hidden route. The journey is presented as a small chapter within a much larger adventure. The player never reaches Lanka. Instead, the game focuses on making the player understand the scale of the journey ahead.

---

## Start Screen

The game opens with a visually appealing title screen.

The player sees:

* Game logo
* Start button
* Adventure-themed background

The only action available is starting the journey.

---

## Journey Map

After pressing Start, the player is shown an ancient map.

The map displays:

* The starting location
* Multiple regions
* Rivers, mountains, forests, and other landmarks
* Lanka as the final destination

The entire route is visible.

The purpose of this screen is not navigation.

The purpose is to communicate:

"We are here."

"Lanka is very far away."

The player is given a Continue button.

---

## Entering the Play Area

When Continue is pressed, the player enters the playable section of the journey.

The playable area represents only a small portion of the complete route shown on the map.

The player is not explicitly told where the playable area begins or ends.

---

## Hidden Exploration System

The game world is initially covered by clouds.

Only:

* Ravan's current location
* Nearby reachable locations

are visible.

The player can only interact with visible reachable locations.

---

## Movement System

When the player selects a reachable location:

* Ravan travels to that location.
* The surrounding clouds are removed.
* New nearby locations become visible.
* Additional choices become available.

The player gradually uncovers more of the map.

---

## Exploration Loop

The entire gameplay experience revolves around:

Observe → Choose → Move → Reveal → Repeat

The player continuously makes decisions about which visible path to explore.

Every movement reveals more of the hidden world.

---

## Dead Ends

Some routes eventually stop.

These routes are not treated as failures.

Instead, they lead to environmental discoveries such as:

* Waterfalls
* Dense forests
* Cliffs
* Ancient structures
* Blocked passages
* Natural landmarks

When the player reaches one of these locations, they understand that the path cannot continue.

The player then returns and explores another route.

---

## Correct Route

One route continues deeper into the journey.

The player discovers this route naturally through exploration.

The game never explicitly marks the correct path.

The player learns through observation and discovery.

---

## Environmental Progression

As the player moves further through the world:

* Forests become denser
* Terrain becomes rougher
* Elevation increases
* Rocky areas begin appearing
* Snowy mountain environments emerge

This progression visually communicates that significant distance has been travelled.

---

## Completion Point

At a specific destination node, the exploration chapter ends.

The game does not build up to this ending.

The ending occurs immediately upon reaching the final node.

This abrupt ending is intentional.

It reinforces the idea that the overall journey is far from complete.

---

## Result Screen

The player is shown a large cinematic illustration.

Ravan is viewed from behind.

In the distance, Lanka can be seen.

The player realizes:

* Progress has been made.
* The destination still remains far away.
* This was only one part of a much larger journey.
